Lynnette: Designing Interface of Intelligent Tutor

Duration: 10 weeks (2022.06-2022.08)
Role: UI/UX design and Research REU intern
Tool: Figma, User Research, Interaction Design
Advisor: Dr. Vincent Aleven, Tomohiro Nagashima
Lynnette is an AI-based tutoring system that provides gamified math learning experience toward middle school students. By enabling students to learn algebra as if they are playing a game that invites students to be a space traveller, Lynnette helps students to become more motivated and engaged during the process of learning. In prior research, Dr. Aleven and his team have found out the drag and drop and gamified component, such as space travel theme, of Lynnette enhances the effectiveness of learning for students. Over the summer, I worked in Dr. Alevenâ€™s lab under the supervision of Tomohiro Nagashima to design and implement the achievements component of Lynnette, which will further enhance its gamified aspect and create even more engaging learning environment for students. Beginning with a body of literature on Intelligent tutoring systems, I designed and prototyped achievements page, its user flow and interaction within Lynnette, and used the think-aloud protocol to test its usability with middle school students.
